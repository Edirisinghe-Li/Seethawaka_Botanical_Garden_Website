The quiet place in the forest for those who love sceanery.

Seethawaka Botanical Garden, established in 2014 in Ilukovita near Avissawella, was developed by Sri Lanka’s Department of National Botanic Gardens to conserve rare and endangered wet zone plant species, especially those from the Sinharaja rainforest. Spanning 79 acres, the garden stands on historic land once part of the Seethawaka Kingdom and now serves as a sanctuary for biodiversity and human wellness. With its serene lakes, shaded walking paths, and lush plant life, the garden offers more than botanical beauty—it provides a peaceful retreat where visitors can relax, reconnect with nature, and gently heal both body and mind.

Seethawaka Botanical Garden offers a variety of services to make each visitor’s experience enjoyable and memorable. Tourists can explore scenic walking trails surrounded by rare and native plant species, take relaxing boat rides on the garden’s tranquil lake, or enjoy a guided tour to learn about the unique biodiversity of Sri Lanka’s wet zone. For added comfort, golf cart rides are available for those who prefer not to walk long distances. The garden also provides photography opportunities, picnic areas, clean restrooms, and information points, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers, families, students, and anyone seeking peace and relaxation in a natural setting.
